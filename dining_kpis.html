Cornell Dining KPIs

A BI Solution for Cornell Dining Services

  1. Home

Time: August – December 2016

Client: Cornell Dining Services

Team: 4 Cornell Graduate Students

Role: BI Designer/Developer

Tools: Data Warehousing Dimensional Modeling, ETL, Dashboard Business Reporting, Tableau, Oracle SQL Developer, WhereScape RED, Oracle, SQL, AWS Workspaces

Project e-Portfolio: https://cornell.digication.com/info_4940_group_3_dining_kpis/Home

Overview:

This project provides Cornell Dining Service ($54M business) with a dashboard to access information regarding four key performance indicators (KPIs) on AYCTE and retail dining: cost of goods sold, inventory turns, sales per labor hour, and average check. This dashboard enables Cornell Dining managers to visualize these KPIs on the units they manage and compare them with Budgeted Values and Industry Standards to measure operational performance and assess the overall health of their organization. Responsibilities include:

  • Gathered the business process requirements by interviewing the clients
  • Developed conceptual dimensional models, extracted, transformed and loaded various of tables to instantiate and populate the data models, created source to target mappings using Oracle SQL Developer and WhereScape RED
  • Designed and implemented dashboards and report worksheets to provide visual representation of the data using Tableau
  • Gained the business insights by analysis of the dashboards and reports
